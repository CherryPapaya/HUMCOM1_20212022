Jeremiah Mateo's totally comprehensive guide on how to talk to a dog

______________

We've all been there. We'd love to talk to a dog but don't know how. Well, fret not. For I shall share my "expertise" with you, dear reader.

You will need

  • Dog
  • Voice
    • You don't need a language.
    • You just need a variety of tones.
  • Dog food
    • Please consult vet for appropriate safe dog food.
    • Please do not treat yourself.
  • A lot of patience
  • Cat (optional)

Why you need the aforementioned

Dog
This should be obvious. To talk to a dog, you will need a dog to talk to.

Voice
Unfortunately, dogs cannot read. Hence, you will need a voice. You do not necessarily need to speak a language as dogs mainly understand tone.

Dog food
Dogs, and any living thing in general, are easier to work with when they have motivation. In this case, the dog's motivation is the promise of food.

A lot of patience
Please remember that dogs are not as intelligent as humans. You will struggle a lot. But please do not give up as dogs make great companions.

Cat (optional)
Cats are intimidating. This can be taken advantage of to keep the dog in check.

Steps

  1. Go up to dog.
    • If you have a cat, keep it at the ready.

  2. Assert your dominance.
    • You can do this by talking with a firm voice while maintaining eye contact.
    • Please do not harm the dog.
    • Assert dominance in a loving-parent kind of way.

  3. Start ushering simple one-syllable commands such as "sit" and "stand".

  4. Repeat steps 1-3 until the dog communicates back.

  5. If the dog communicates, reward the good boy/girl with a treat.
